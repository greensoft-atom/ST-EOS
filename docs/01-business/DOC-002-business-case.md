## 1. Executive Summary

ST-EOS addresses a structural inefficiency in science and technology executive organizations: critical knowledge is trapped in documents, while staff perform repetitive review, drafting, and evaluation tasks manually. By combining an existing local LLM capability with a domain-specific document corpus and workflow-oriented AI services, ST-EOS can reduce processing time, improve decision consistency, and preserve institutional memory — while meeting data sovereignty requirements that exclude most commercial AI products.

**Recommendation:** Approve Phase 0 discovery and Phase 1 MVP investment with one anchor pilot institution.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

### 2.1 Current state challenges

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Document overload | Review cycles take weeks; bottlenecks in committees |
| Knowledge fragmentation | Past projects, patents, and reports are hard to find and compare |
| Inconsistent evaluation | Different reviewers apply different standards |
| Drafting burden | Proposals, plans, and reports built from scratch |
| Generic AI unsuitable | Cloud tools lack domain corpus and local deployment |
| Technology scouting ad hoc | No systematic way to scan internal and external signals |

### 2.2 Who is affected

- Executive directors and planning units
- Research offices and grant administrators
- Patent and IP staff
- Innovation and exhibition committees
- Evaluation and audit units
- Technology transfer offices

---

## 3. Proposed Solution

An integrated platform (ST-EOS) providing:

| Capability | Business value |
|------------|----------------|
| Institutional knowledge search | Faster research, less duplication |
| AI document review | Shorter review cycles, fewer omissions |
| Template-based creation | Standardized outputs, less rework |
| Evaluation assistance | Fairer, traceable scoring support |
| Department assistants | Specialized support without hiring surge |
| Local deployment | Compliance with government data policies |

---

## 4. Alternatives Considered

| Alternative | Pros | Cons | Decision |
|-------------|------|------|----------|
| **Do nothing** | No cost | Pain persists; competitors may adopt AI | Rejected |
| **Generic ChatGPT/Claude (cloud)** | Fast to start | Data leaves premises; no institutional corpus | Rejected as primary |
| **Commercial DMS only** | Document storage | No intelligent workflow | Insufficient |
| **Build ST-EOS** | Domain fit, local AI, expandable | Upfront investment | **Selected** |
| **Buy vertical SaaS (e.g., grant system)** | Proven workflows | May not cover full S&T scope; cloud | Partial complement only |

## 7. Return on Investment (Illustrative)

Assume pilot institution with 50 knowledge workers spending 20% of time on document/search/evaluation tasks.

| Assumption | Value |
|------------|-------|
| Staff count | 50 |
| Average fully loaded cost | Organization-specific |
| Time on target tasks | 20% |
| Efficiency gain (conservative) | 15% of that time |
| **Effective recovery** | ~1.5 FTE equivalent |

For larger ministries or national agencies, recovery scales significantly. ROI improves as modules expand beyond MVP.

**Note:** ROI should be recalculated with pilot institution actual salary and workflow data.

## 8. Market & Strategic Context

### 8.1 Target market segments

1. **Government** — National/ministry S&T departments
2. **Research** — Universities, national labs
3. **Innovation** — Tech parks, incubators, exhibition bodies
4. **Industry** — Corporate R&D and IP departments

### 8.2 Strategic positioning

ST-EOS is not competing with general AI assistants. It competes with:

- Manual processes
- Fragmented document archives
- Disconnected grant/patent/exhibition systems without AI layer

**Differentiator:** Domain corpus + local LLM + executive workflow modules.

---

## 9. Risks to Business Case

| Risk | Effect on ROI | Mitigation |
|------|---------------|------------|
| Low adoption | Benefits unrealized | Pilot champions, task-based UX |
| AI quality insufficient | Rework increases | PoC gate, human-in-loop |
| Long sales cycle (gov) | Delayed revenue | Anchor pilot first |
| Competitor enters | Margin pressure | Deep domain moat, on-prem |

## 10. Recommendation

Proceed with ST-EOS in phased investment:

1. **Phase 0** — Low-cost PoC to validate RAG and review quality
2. **Phase 1** — MVP build only if PoC passes
3. **Phase 2** — Scale after pilot metrics confirm value

**Decision requested:** Approve charter, nominate sponsor, commit pilot institution.
